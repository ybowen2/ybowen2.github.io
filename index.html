Corals & Birds

I think slowly

Little by little, things do work out.

If we light fires, they stay ignited. The warmth grows. You can see it in the eyes of the burning ones. They cherish and they surpass the turning of the tides

Because sometimes the tides do turn.

They turn further.

Harder

Longer

For some of us.

Making some of us struggle to find our match. We struggle to find the same fires and we struggle against the tides.

Yet most times we forget to look under the water.

Being a coral is just as beautiful as being a bird

. There is color, art and meaning.

We may live under the control of the tides but it is not fair to us to have to envy those on solid ground. At every stage of our existence there is either water or solid ground. Some of us can stay afloat or we can be immersed. Or maybe we are the lucky ones with the gravel on our paths.

Yet we all live this one life.

To experience just the good or just the bad times is to have lived unfulfilled

I'd like to take my chances with turning tides. I'd trust them to wash me ashore someday and until that day,

I'd like to be a pretty coral.

When the Mind and Heart Converse

When the Mind and Heart Converse
Summer is a breeze, a gentle romance Rest my beating heart: take a stance As you dig your grave stay awhile Look at what the tide brings to shore Be waist deep in your thoughtless ignorance But be pure.
You are one with your companion My soul, my voice, my stronghold Yet you both see not what love has foretold Trust wavers and golden glories fade You are left jaded, scarred, broken, charmed Yet through it all you have stayed Breathing.
So regardless of what fails, Breathe Dust, pollen, smog, the scent of a jasmine Breathe For what arrives will pass careless of your grasp Take in it's scent and let it leave it's mark Shall it poison your core? Shall you be ripped? Or shall it shine on you the light of a billion stars? I do know. I cannot know and neither can you Yet after it has passed, you will yet breathe And if you do not It will never again worry you.

Sunrise and Sunset

I sit facing east under the tree that I call home I am no more than a youth old I feel the breeze whip through my hair: passing feverishly. It's strength is strong, sharp and unreal. Yet it pleases me because it is free and so am I. The leaves above me are bright. The sun peeps through their little imperfect crochet nets. The grass below me is brighter still. The soft touch of it's moist blades soften the day's glow.  I can feel my cheeks blush and I smile for no particular reason. It is a time when I have memories enough to cherish. I have a present strong enough to trust the future blindly. I smile softer at my own blushing and I wonder. I wonder why I have bruises on my knees and none on my soul. I am glad that my eyes are light and my spirit is bold. I wonder why our red hearts love so deep. I am my hope. Sunrise is gold. She sits facing west under the tree that she calls home She is no more than a lifetime old She feels the breeze whip through her hair but she doesn't move. Its strength is strong, sharp and real. Yet she likes it because it hurts and so does she. The leaves above her are wrinkled. They lower their heads as they are fated to fall. The ground below her is wrinkled harder still. The cracks along it are rough to her touch. She feels her blood rise and she sheds a tear for no particular reason. It is a time when she has regrets enough to anguish upon. She has a past strong enough to banish the future blindly. She tears harder at her own fate and wonders She wonders why her knees are soft but she bruised her soul. She is sad her spirit fails her and the heart cannot hold. She wonders why our red eyes cry so deep She is my hope lost. Yet Sunset too is gold.

Questions Between Lines

Can we truly know what love is worth our loyalty?
If I surrender to a love that makes my heart burst with agony and grows flames in my insides Do I call that true love or passion that will one day be fruitless?
If I surrender to a love that takes a few years to blossom while it gradually strengthens it's foothold and makes me feel safe Do I call it true love or a resigned settlement?
If I test my youth against my wisdom Would it direct me to a fool's lust or to a companion's care?
If I test my adoration against my carnal instinct would it direct me to my short lived bliss or to my long aching stargaze?
Can I remember all that I have to give and put everything in a tiny jar that is too incapable of holding its contents and empty them out with gladness into the life of the best friend I love even though I receive nothing in return? Can I take back that empty jar and fill it with the smiles he will one day give another ? Would that jar of happiness be mine or his If only I cry?
